§ 02 Copilot strategy

Buy Copilot for the people it will actually change.

At $30 per user per month, Copilot is a serious line item — and most companies waste half of it on people whose work it doesn't touch. Here's how we decide, role by role.

✓ Worth buying for

People whose day is words, decks, spreadsheets, or email volume.

  • 01 Owners and executives who write, decide, and communicate all day — Outlook and Word Copilot pay for themselves in the first month.
  • 02 Analysts and finance staff living in Excel — natural-language formulas and data insights are transformative.
  • 03 Salespeople and BD leads writing dozens of tailored proposals, follow-ups, and briefs per week.
  • 04 Attorneys, consultants, and knowledge workers whose output is documents, and whose input is meetings.
  • 05 Marketing and content leads shipping PowerPoint, newsletters, and long-form regularly.
✕ Skip Copilot for

People whose work happens outside the Office apps.

  • 01 Field staff, drivers, technicians — their tools are phones, forms, and dispatch, not Word documents.
  • 02 Retail and hourly frontline — Frontline F1/F3 licenses cover their needs at a fraction of the cost.
  • 03 Occasional users who mostly read email and open the odd spreadsheet — free Copilot Chat is plenty.
  • 04 Anyone who won't be trained — without prompt fluency, Copilot becomes an expensive spellchecker.
  • 05 Teams without clean SharePoint or OneDrive — Copilot's power comes from your data. Fix the storage first.

“A 20-person firm doesn't need 20 Copilot licenses. It needs five, aimed at the people who move the business — and Business Premium for everyone else. That's a $9,000/year decision made correctly.”

— GrowthPoint engagement notes
§ 03 SharePoint

Make SharePoint a business asset — not a file dump.

Most organizations use SharePoint the way they used a shared drive in 2008: nested folders, dead links, and one person who knows where anything actually lives. Copilot can't fix that. Structure can — and once structure is in place, Copilot becomes an oracle.

How we structure it

Layer 04
Copilot & Search
Semantic answers pulled from properly-tagged content across the tenant.
Layer 03
Knowledge Hubs
Topic-based hubs with curated pages, not just document libraries.
Layer 02
Team & Project Sites
Governed sites with metadata, retention, and clear ownership.
Layer 01
Document Foundation
Consistent libraries, sensitivity labels, versioning, permissions.

Structured content is the foundation of every useful AI feature you own.

When we redesign a tenant, we start with the boring layer no vendor will sell you: metadata, permissions, and information architecture. That's what turns Copilot from a novelty into an internal expert.

  1. 01
    Intranet + landing page that people actually open
    A curated homepage with news, policies, and quick links. Not a directory of team sites nobody visits.
  2. 02
    Document libraries with metadata, not folders
    Tag by client, project, status, and sensitivity. Filtered views replace hunting through folder trees.
  3. 03
    Copilot-ready knowledge hubs
    Once content is tagged and permissioned correctly, Copilot Chat answers real questions — "what's our policy on…", "find the latest version of…"
  4. 04
    Search that returns the right result first
    Promoted results, refiners, and topic pages that make internal search feel like Google — because most of it is the same technology.
  5. 05
    Governance without the bureaucracy
    Site lifecycle, external sharing controls, and retention policies calibrated for a small business — not a bank.

§ 05 Business patterns

How we configure it, by business type.

Every engagement is different, but patterns repeat. Here's what a right-sized Microsoft 365 + Copilot + SharePoint configuration usually looks like for the organizations we work with most.

01
Independent consultant or coach
Business Standard + Copilot Chat (free). SharePoint used for client folders with metadata; OneDrive for personal work.
Where the impact lands: proposal drafts, meeting recaps, and Excel modeling for pricing scenarios.
02
Insurance or real-estate agency (5–15 people)
Business Standard for the team + 1–2 Copilot seats for owner and top producer.
Where the impact lands: policy comparisons, listing descriptions, follow-up sequences, and Excel commission tracking.
03
Small law firm or accounting practice
Business Premium across the board + Copilot for partners and senior associates. Defender + DLP essential.
Where the impact lands: brief and memo drafting, deposition summaries, engagement letters, and secure client portals.
04
Nonprofit (10–40 staff)
Business Basic or Premium via nonprofit pricing. Copilot for ED and development director only.
Where the impact lands: grant writing, donor communications, and board packets. SharePoint replaces the drive-of-lost-things.
05
Growing service business (25–75)
Business Premium everywhere + Copilot for operations, sales, and leadership. Power Automate for approvals.
Where the impact lands: proposal turnaround, ops reporting, onboarding checklists, and a real intranet employees actually visit.
06
Government contractor / regulated
Microsoft 365 E5 or GCC + Copilot selectively + full compliance stack (Purview, Insider Risk, DLP).
Where the impact lands: CMMC posture, audit trails, controlled data-handling — with productivity kept intact.
07
Church, ministry, or community org
Business Basic (nonprofit) + SharePoint for volunteer resources. Copilot rarely justified.
Where the impact lands: shared calendars, volunteer schedules, sermon and event archives, simple internal search.
